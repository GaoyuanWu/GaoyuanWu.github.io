This winter I travelled to Japan with my parents and girlfriend. We went to Hokkaido and Honshu Island. From snowy Sapporo to windy Kamakura, from Kyoto shrines to Tokyo skyscrapers, I deeply felt the 
beauty of this civilized country. Japan is blended with traditional assets and modern technology, with natural resources and artificial manufacture, with external reserve and hidden craziness. Very attractive.

>Hokkaido

Hokkaido is a place that can intepret my imagination of the World of Snow. It has both modern cities like Sapporo and quiet towns like Toya. People here
enjoy beer, ski and seafood.

>Honshu

The famous Osaka, Kyoto & Tokyo are all in Honshu. Big cities like Tokyo are much more crowded than Hokkaido little towns. However, Japanese traditions are well preserved in Honshu.
You can explore shrines, temples, the culture of Kimoni and Japanese food in Honshu. Last minute you may be walking along thousands of people at crossroad in Shibuya, Tokyo, the next
minute you may say your prayer in the temples. Marvelous experience!
